# CryptSweeper 500-card expansion

The implementation-ready record for every proposed card is
[`card-expansion-500.json`](./card-expansion-500.json). Each record contains the
name, engine rarity, design tier, cost, base and upgraded rules text, existing
mechanics used, archetype, value assessment, existing entry route, keep/remove
reasoning, example, balance risk, tuning range, and overlap note.

The source definitions are in
[`src/engine/cardExpansion500.js`](../../src/engine/cardExpansion500.js). The
catalog is regenerated with `node scripts/generate-card-expansion-report.mjs`.

## Existing mechanical inventory

Only the following pre-existing systems are used:

- Card classifications: Attack, Skill, Power, Status, and Curse. The expansion
  adds no new type. Its showcase tier is mechanically Rare, not a new rarity.
- Reward rarities: Common, Uncommon, and Rare; `starter` and `special` remain
  reserved for starter cards and established Status/Curse burdens.
- Costs and cycling: Energy costs from 0–3, draw, normal discard after play,
  Exhaust, and unplayable cards. The engine has no general transform or
  player-directed discard-card verb, so the expansion does not claim either.
- Player resources: Health, Block, Plating, Energy, Insight, Picks, and Max
  Picks. Gold remains outside combat and is not generated by these cards.
- Tile information/actions: Reveal, Scan, Flag, Verified Flag, Chord, Defuse,
  Detonate, Entomb, and Swap.
- Existing board values: hidden/revealed state, safe tiles, mines, flags,
  scanned state, a revealed tile’s number, tiles revealed this turn, cascade
  size, rows, adjacent tiles, and current board mine/flag counts.
- Existing board editing: safe or mixed edge annexation, adding a mine, and the
  existing Sentry/Relay constructs. No new tile or construct kind is added.
- Existing combat output: targeted, random-enemy, and all-enemy damage.
- Existing safety/risk rules: Instinct, normal mine damage, controlled
  detonation, Full Clear, and the established interaction between Block,
  Plating, and mines.

## Existing pool audit

Before this expansion the catalog contained 200 cards: 101 Common, 55
Uncommon, 35 Rare, five Starters, and four Special cards. Attacks and Skills
dominated; only five Powers existed. Costs were usually 0–3 Energy, ordinary
damage generally sat around 4–16, premium attacks reached the twenties, and
defense generally sat around 4–16. Exhaust, multi-target tile control, numbered
tile targeting, row targeting, adjacency, Swap, Entomb, annexation, constructs,
and Max Pick trades were comparatively rare.

Common patterns were “Reveal then attack,” “Scan plus utility,” “Defuse or
Detonate for damage,” flat Block/Plating, damage per flag/scan/reveal, and a
small set of class Powers. The established archetypes were Sapper mines,
Surveyor scans/Insight, Terraformer board editing/constructs, Lamplighter
cascades, Gambler flags, Chirurgeon damage recovery, Archivist draw/Exhaust,
Warden defense, Hexwright numbers, and Revenant high-risk offense.

Effects that should remain rare are mass guaranteed resolution, multiple
Entombs, mass Verified Flags, Energy plus draw on one card, large all-enemy
scaling, and multi-turn constructs. Free draw combined with Energy refunds,
repeatable Pick generation, unlimited flag scaling, and non-Exhausting mass
resolution are the main deterministic-win or excessive-control risks.

Deck management currently consists of normal end-turn discard, draw/discard
reshuffling, combat-only Exhaust, card rewards, merchant purchases, camp
upgrades, and escalating merchant removal. There is no existing transformation
or direct discard-selection system.

## Archetype groups

Each Delver receives a 45-card cycle. The cycles share readable base structures,
but they are not number-only mirrors: every card also receives one of three
class-specific conditional riders built from existing mechanics. Sapper cards
care about detonations, verified flags, or mine density; Surveyor cards add
scans, Insight, or scanned-mine defense; Terraformer cards count Constructs or
Entombs; Lamplighter cards branch on tiles revealed by the card; Gambler cards
need flag thresholds; Chirurgeon cards respond to low Block/Plating; Archivist
cards draw conditionally or Exhaust; Warden cards convert Block and Plating;
Hexwright cards read the highest revealed number; and Revenant cards voluntarily
trade 1 HP for Energy, Block, or Picks. This produces 450 distinct class/recipe
combinations without introducing a new class rule.

- Safe-frontier and cascade: Measured Strike, Momentum Blow, Blind Advance,
  Safe Perimeter, Cascade Dividend, Open Ledger, and Clear Ground.
- Numbered tiles and exact deduction: Numbered Brace, Honest Mark, Neighbor
  Notes, Chord Lesson, Exact Count, Grand Survey, Adaptive Works, and Perfect
  Deduction.
- Mines, flags, and controlled risk: Mine Dividend, Flag Salvo, Salvage Charge,
  Controlled Chain, Mine Investment, Known Risk, Flag Economy, Mine Symphony,
  Dense Ground, Verified Arsenal, Reversal, and Mine Harvest.
- Board shape and resolution: Peripheral Survey, Quiet Burial, Row Check, Fault
  Reading, Edge Claim, Clean Sequence, Full-Clear Aspirant, Stone Exchange,
  Cartographer’s Coup, Controlled Collapse, and Last Map.
- Defense, consistency, and cycling: Surveyed Guard, Trail Reserve, Plate
  Exchange, Deliberate Effort, Watchful Sentry, Patient Relay, and Last Pick.
- Build-arounds: Verified Arsenal/Harvest for verified flags; Numbered
  Brace/Grand Survey/Perfect Deduction for numbers; Sentry/Relay/Adaptive Works
  for constructs; Full-Clear Aspirant/Controlled Collapse for Entomb; Mine
  Investment/Mine Symphony for created mines; Safe Perimeter/Cascade Dividend
  for reveal chains.

## Deck burdens

The 50 burdens use five ten-card families and the already-supported Status and
Curse labels. They are `special` and classless, so normal rewards and shops do
not accidentally offer them. Their catalog metadata assigns only established
event, enemy/board-outcome, risk/reward, merchant-trap, or puzzle-failure entry
routes.

- Bent tools: expensive, weak Block. Emergency value exists, but their draw and
  Energy inefficiency normally justify removal.
- Bad readings: spend Energy to perform a risky normal Reveal. They can preserve
  a Pick after accurate deduction, but bad timing can consume Instinct.
- Loose fill: add and verify a mine for meager Block. Mine/Sapper decks may keep
  one; most decks should remove it.
- Cramped movement: lose a Max Pick for modest Plating. Defensive decks may use
  one, while multiples become increasingly harmful.
- Smudged notes: overpriced single-tile Scans. Surveyor can occasionally use one
  to reach Field Method’s fourth scan.

All burdens Exhaust when played, so the player can spend an existing resource
to clear them for the current combat. None deals unavoidable damage, forces a
target, removes agency, or enters ordinary reward pools.

## Overlap audit

- Measured Strike deliberately overlaps Probe’s Reveal-then-hit structure;
  class tuning and reward rarity are its only differentiation. It is the first
  candidate to cut if reward pools feel repetitive.
- Known Risk parallels Short Fuse but demands a prior Scan rather than testing
  the tile immediately.
- Mine Symphony parallels Chain Charge and should be compared directly during
  Sapper tests.
- Flag Economy parallels Munitions Cache but pays more Energy and replaces
  itself.
- Clean Sequence and Last Map parallel one another at single- versus
  multi-target scale; Last Map’s cost and Exhaust are essential.
- Watchful Sentry and Patient Relay reuse existing constructs instead of adding
  new board pieces. Their cantrip/defense riders must not eclipse the original
  Sentry and Survey Relay.
- Full-Clear Aspirant parallels Entomb but trades efficiency for multi-target
  control and Exhaust.
- The 10 cards inside each burden family are intentionally close variants. This
  creates a broad event-facing burden pool without multiplying new rules.

## Combo and dominant-strategy risks

No card creates a formal infinite loop by itself. The main watch list is:

1. Last Pick plus zero-cost Pick spenders and draw can lengthen turns. It
   requires zero Picks and Exhausts, preventing direct repetition.
2. Perfect Deduction refunds Energy and draws cards. Exhaust and a demanding
   exact-flag condition are required safeguards.
3. Flag Economy into Verified Arsenal or Mine Harvest may yield deterministic
   damage and board control. Tune flag count before damage if dominant.
4. Multiple constructs can make long combats automatic. Existing board attacks
   destroy constructs, but construct density still needs boss testing.
5. Entomb cycles can approach forced Full Clears. Multi-Entomb cards therefore
   cost at least 2 Energy and Exhaust.
6. Flag-scaling damage must stay capped because placing manual flags is free.
7. Surveyor triggers during mass Scan can refund Energy. Grand Survey’s refund
   cap and showcase Exhaust cards prevent open-ended chains.
8. Class riders compound the base recipe. In particular, Surveyor mass Scans,
   Archivist conditional draw, Lamplighter Energy refunds, and Revenant’s
   voluntary HP trades should be evaluated as complete cards rather than tuning
   the base recipe in isolation.

## Burden severity audit

- Potentially too harmless: upgraded Bent tools and Smudged notes for Surveyor.
  Weaker variant: keep upgrade cost unchanged. Stronger burden variant: reduce
  Block by 1 or make the Scan cost 2 at both upgrade states.
- Potentially too punishing: multiple Cramped movement cards. Do not grant more
  than one from a single event and cap burden acquisition at three per run.
- Potentially beneficial: Loose fill in Sapper/verified-flag decks. This is
  intentional narrow value; reduce Block to 2 if it becomes an automatic keep.
- Closest to always-remove: Bent tools. Raising base Block from 2–3 to 4 makes
  them situational; lowering it creates a purer deck-cleaning pressure card.
- Closest to always-keep in one class: Smudged notes for Surveyor. A fixed
  2-Energy cost is the harsher variant.

## First playtest tranche

Add these recipe cycles first because they test underused systems without the
highest control risk:

1. Numbered Brace and Neighbor Notes.
2. Honest Mark and Chord Lesson.
3. Fault Reading and Reversal.
4. Plate Exchange and Last Pick.
5. Edge Claim and Adaptive Works.
6. One card from each burden family.

Hold Cartographer’s Coup, Controlled Collapse, Perfect Deduction, Mine Harvest,
and Last Map for the second tranche after normal Rare baselines are measured.

## Tuning variants

- Mass Scan: weaker scans 3–4 tiles; stronger scans a full neighborhood. Never
  combine the stronger version with an uncapped Energy refund.
- Multi-Entomb: weaker Entombs one tile and draws; stronger Entombs three and
  Exhausts. Do not make the stronger version cost below 2.
- Verified-flag payoff: weaker 3 damage per mine; stronger 5–6. Reduce the mine
  cap before raising damage.
- Cascade payoff: weaker 2 damage per revealed tile with a 16 cap; stronger 3
  with a 24 cap. Caps are safer than changing cascade rules.
- Last Pick: weaker gains 2 Picks/draws 1; stronger gains 3/draws 2. Both must
  require zero Picks and Exhaust.
- Burden families have their individual tuning ranges in the JSON catalog.

## Removal economy

Merchant removal begins at 75 Gold and increases by 25 after each use. Because
removal competes with cards, trinkets, and gadgets, that economy supports roughly
one deliberate burden removal comfortably and a second at a meaningful cost. It
does **not** support frequent acquisition merely because 50 burden definitions
exist. Initial event integration should average no more than one or two burdens
per run, never grant more than one copy from a single outcome, and hard-cap a run
at three newly acquired burdens until telemetry shows sufficient shop access and
Gold. If average acquisition rises, add more existing-system removal
opportunities or flatten removal escalation before increasing burden frequency.

## Compatibility confirmation

Every implemented effect is composed exclusively from engine verbs that existed
before this expansion. The validation suite checks allowed card types, rarities,
targets, costs, metadata completeness, exact distribution, unique names, and
callable rules/play functions. No new resource, status behavior, board element,
currency, card type, keyword, or combat rule is introduced.
