# Fiction-first events

CryptSweeper uses a deliberately small authored event deck. An event is a
physical situation in the Undermine, not a lesson or a disguised multiple-choice
question. Its costs, rewards, uncertainty, and delayed consequences must follow
from objects and people present in the scene.

The live deck contains 20 event cards:

| Event | Consequence carried by the fiction |
| --- | --- |
| The Two-Door Shrine | One sealed door contains offerings; the other contains a funeral charge. |
| The Cartographer's Corpse | Maps bring a gadget and the dead cartographer's fear; burial builds resolve. |
| The Rat's Three Doors | Sell a claim safely or trust the Rat's informed reveal. |
| The Prisoners' Bargain | Share a chained cache or seize it and meet the prisoner again. |
| The Alchemist's Red Test | Quarantine costs supplies; confirmation costs coin and can permit early treatment. |
| The Common Mushroom Bed | A careful harvest heals; stripping the shared bed enriches and stains the deck. |
| The Flooded Stair | Rescue releases the flood; retreat preserves the delver and abandons the miner. |
| The Begging Shade | Pay a funeral debt for resolve or rob the bowl and inherit its haunting. |
| The Twin Idols | Blood tempers a card; salt and gold close wounds. |
| The Ossuary Game | A genuinely blind bone wager pays coin or takes blood. |
| The Bell-Toll Door | Pay the toll now or let its keeper collect a named debt later. |
| The Cavern Hound | Treat the trapped animal or steal its pack and face it again. |
| The Glowcap Feast | Eat the suspicious funeral meal or burn it and rob the dead diners. |
| The Echoing Choir | Sustain the sealing note at bodily cost or break it for the offerings. |
| The Marked Ledger | Expose stolen wages or sign for a corrupt payment. |
| The Groaning Vault | Take safe near salvage or crawl deeper while the structure collapses. |
| The Last Signal Fire | Spend light to rescue stranded miners or preserve it for the route. |
| The Fraying Rope | Hold a rival's weight or cut them loose and take what remains. |
| The Wounded Rival | Rescue a competitor and split the route, or steal it and answer later. |
| The Unmaking Font | The font's lie is mechanical: the chosen card returns doubled. |

Nine events can return later. Their follow-ups are written for the original
person, promise, or location; there is no generic reward/debt callback.

## Authoring rule

Every new event must answer three questions before it enters the catalog:

1. What visible thing causes each mechanical consequence?
2. What does the player understand without an academic explanation?
3. If it returns later, who or what remembered the choice?
